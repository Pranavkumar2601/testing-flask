Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams, each consisting of eleven players. The game is played on a field with a 22-yard rectangular pitch at the center. Each end of the pitch features a wicket, composed of two bails balanced on three stumps1. The objective of the game is to score more runs than the opposing team. A match is divided into innings during which one team bats and the other bowls2. The batting team tries to score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets, while the bowling team attempts to restrict runs and dismiss the batters1. Cricket is popular worldwide, particularly in countries of the Commonwealth, and is governed by the International Cricket Council1. It has various formats including Test matches, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 Internationals1. Each format requires different strategies and skills, adding to the complexity and appeal of the game1.
